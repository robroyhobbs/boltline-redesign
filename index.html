Chapter 1

We started with the current site.

Boltline has one of the most compelling origin stories in B2B software: a hardware coordination platform built inside Stoke Space by engineers who were literally building reusable rockets. When their existing tools couldn't keep up, they built their own. Proved it on actual rocket hardware. Then spun it out for everyone.

The current site is competent — clean, modern WordPress build, good product page structure, strong case studies. But it's underselling what makes Boltline genuinely different. The website could belong to any SaaS company. It shouldn't.

The core tension: Boltline is a software company born from a rocket company, but the website doesn't feel like it. The origin story is buried. The social proof is below the fold. The messaging leads with features instead of problems.

Chapter 2

Six things holding the site back.

We audited the homepage, product page, use cases, and overall messaging. These aren't cosmetic issues — they're structural problems that affect how buyers perceive Boltline and whether they convert.

1

Customer logos are buried below the fold

Intuitive Machines is building NASA's lunar lander with Boltline. True Anomaly, iSpace, XCIMER — these are serious companies. But a visitor has to scroll past walls of text to find them. Social proof should be immediate.

2

The Stoke Space origin story is barely visible

"Built by rocket engineers, proven on actual rocket hardware" is a competitive moat that no competitor can replicate. Currently it's a footnote. It should be impossible to miss.

3

Industries instead of use cases

The site frames use cases by industry ("Space & Defense," "Medical & Biotech"). But buyers search for problems: "hardware BOM management software," not "space and defense solutions." Problem-led framing matches how people actually buy.

4

Walls of text in the 5-step workflow

The Design → Build → Test → Trace → Improve lifecycle is the right framework. But each step gets a full paragraph where a single sentence would land harder. Density kills scanability.

5

Single CTA path: "Book a Demo" only

Not every visitor is ready for a demo. There's no mid-funnel offer — no checklist, no ROI calculator, no implementation guide. The site captures high-intent visitors but loses everyone else.

6

The design doesn't match the story

This is a software company spun out of a rocket company, backed by Toyota's venture fund, trusted by lunar mission teams. The current WordPress theme is clean but generic. The design should make you feel the origin story.

The Shift We're Proposing

Current Site
  • Feature-first messaging
  • Industry-framed use cases
  • Logos below the fold
  • Origin story in footer/about
  • Demo-only conversion path
  • Generic SaaS aesthetic
What We Built
  • Problem-first headlines
  • Buyer-problem use cases
  • Logos above the fold
  • Origin story front and center
  • Demo + mid-funnel offers
  • Design worthy of rockets
Chapter 3

Same problems. Five different philosophies.

Rather than prescribing one direction, we built five distinct homepage concepts. Each one solves the same six problems but through a completely different design lens. This gives the team real options to react to — not just one mock to approve or reject.

All five use Boltline's actual branding, real customer logos, real product screenshots, and real factory photography from boltline.com.

CONCEPT 01

Mission Control

Dark, aerospace-forward. Like SpaceX launch control crossed with Linear. Neon purple glows, glass-morphism cards, constellation particles. The whole site feels like mission software.

Leans into: Aerospace heritage, technical credibility, premium feel
CONCEPT 02

Clean Engineering

Minimal, Stripe-inspired precision. Bright white, massive whitespace, product UI mockups with real BOM data. The anti-enterprise-software feel.

Leans into: Modern SaaS credibility, product clarity, simplicity
CONCEPT 03

Origin Story

Cinematic, scroll-driven narrative. Starts dark in the rocket factory with amber sparks, transitions to bright modern software. The homepage IS the founding story.

Leans into: Emotional connection, brand differentiation, storytelling
CONCEPT 04

Product-Led

Show-don't-tell. The product is the hero with interactive tabbed workflows showing 5 different dashboard views. The visitor can almost use Boltline from the homepage.

Leans into: Product-led growth, transparency, technical buyers
CONCEPT 05

Category Creator

Bold, provocative positioning. Giant typography, contrarian headlines: "Not PLM. Not MES. Hardware, coordinated." The Figma of hardware platforms.

Leans into: Category ownership, brand confidence, market positioning
